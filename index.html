Abstract

We calibrate a camera to a LiDAR by working entirely in a 2D projective LiDAR render. Our key observation is that rendering the LiDAR point cloud from a virtual pinhole camera at the LiDAR's own optical center—its “eye”—yields an occlusion-free image. We therefore define the calibration objective in this LiDAR-eye render, which makes it well-behaved for global optimization and, uniquely, enables a joint multi-frame objective without per-frame heuristics. We demonstrate accurate, self-consistent calibration and dense overlays across a wide field of view.

Method

Three stages, all in the eye of the LiDAR.

1

Calibrate the LiDAR to its own eye

We first recover the LiDAR's optical center—its “eye”—so the cloud can be rendered from that exact viewpoint.

2

Optimize in the LiDAR-eye render

The calibration objective is defined in the occlusion-free LiDAR-eye render and optimized jointly across frames. The animation shows the alignment converging.

3

Calibrated result & depth render

The calibrated cloud projects cleanly onto the RGB video, staying aligned across the full wide field of view.
